Natural Sciences are the branches of science that study the physical world and everything in it. They focus on things that exist in nature — from tiny atoms to the whole universe. Natural Sciences try to understand how the natural world works using observation, experiments, and evidence.

1. Physical Sciences
These study non-living things like matter and energy.
Physics – Study of matter, energy, motion, and forces
Chemistry – Study of atoms, molecules, and chemical reactions
Astronomy – Study of stars, planets, galaxies, and the universe

2. Earth Sciences
These study planet Earth and its systems.
Geology – Study of rocks, minerals, and Earth’s structure
Meteorology – Study of weather and climate
Oceanography – Study of oceans and marine life
Paleontology – Study of fossils and ancient life

3. Life Sciences (Biology)
These study living things and life processes.
Botany – Study of plants
Zoology – Study of animals
Genetics – Study of genes and DNA
Microbiology – Study of tiny organisms like bacteria and viruses
Ecology – Study of how living things interact with their environment

Natural Sciences are the foundation of science. They help us explain everyday things like why the sky is blue, how medicines work, and how the Earth changes over time.
