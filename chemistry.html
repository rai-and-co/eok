Chemistry is the branch of science that studies matter and how it changes. It explains what things are made of and how they react with each other. Chemistry helps us understand the world at the level of atoms and molecules. It is often called the “central science” because it connects physics with biology.

1. General Chemistry
The study of matter, its properties, and how different substances interact and change.

2. Organic Chemistry
Study of carbon-based compounds (found in living things, fuels, plastics, medicines).

3. Inorganic Chemistry
Study of compounds that do not contain carbon (metals, minerals, salts).

Chemists are scientists who study matter and chemical reactions. They explore how substances are made and how they change. They:
Study atoms and molecules
Investigate chemical reactions
Create new materials and medicines
Test and analyze substances
Solve problems using chemistry

Chemists work in labs, industries, hospitals, and schools. Their discoveries help make medicines, plastics, food, and many everyday products.

Chemistry is everywhere — in cooking, cleaning, medicines, plastics, and even in our own bodies.
