Science is a way of learning and understanding the world around us by asking questions, testing ideas, and finding evidence. It helps us discover how things really work. Science is not just a bunch of facts in books. It is a process that helps us learn new things and correct old mistakes when better evidence appears. Science uses a special step-by-step method called the Scientific Method. Scientists follow this method to make sure their discoveries are reliable and can be checked by others.

Science is divided into different branches so we can study different things:

1. Natural Sciences
These study the natural world (things that exist in nature).
Examples: Physical Sciences, Earth Sciences, Life Sciences

2. Social Sciences
These study people and how they behave and live together.
Examples: Psychology, Sociology, Economics, Anthropology

3. Formal Sciences
These study rules and logic, not physical things.
Examples: Mathematics, Statistics, Computer Science

The Scientific Method is a step-by-step process that scientists use to answer questions and discover reliable knowledge about the world. It helps make sure findings are based on real evidence.

1. Observe
Notice something interesting in the world.

2. Ask a Question
Turn the observation into a clear question.

3. Form a Hypothesis
Make an educated guess that can be tested.

4. Test the Hypothesis
Do an experiment or collect data to check if the guess is correct.

5. Analyze the Results
Look at the data and see what it shows.

6. Draw Conclusions
Decide if the hypothesis is supported or not. If not, change it and test again.

7. Share the Results
Tell others so they can check or repeat the work.

Science is always changing. When new evidence appears, old ideas can be improved or replaced. That is why science is so powerful — it keeps getting better. Science helps us solve problems, invent new things, and understand the universe.
