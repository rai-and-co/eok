Physical Sciences are the branches of science that study non-living things. They focus on matter, energy, forces, and the basic laws of the universe. Physical Sciences help us understand how the physical world works — from tiny particles to the entire universe.

1. Physics
Study of matter, energy, motion, forces, and energy.
Examples: gravity, light, electricity, heat.

2. Chemistry
Study of atoms, molecules, and how they combine or react.
Examples: water, metals, acids, chemical reactions.

Physical Sciences are the foundation for many inventions like phones, cars, electricity, and rockets. They explain everyday things like why objects fall or how fireworks work.
